# Visa Calls Korea "Optimal Country" for Stablecoin Experimentation

**Source:** Seoul Economic Daily — April 5, 2026

## Visit details

- **When:** Late March 2026
- **Who:** Oliver Jenkyn (Visa Group President, Global Markets) and Stephen Karpin (Visa Asia-Pacific President)
- **Meeting:** With top Korean executives including those from Shinhan Financial Group

## Direct quotes from Visa executives

> "Korea has 17 million cryptocurrency investors and ranks second after the United States in paid subscription rates for ChatGPT."

> "We view Korea as the optimal place to experiment with stablecoins outside the United States, and we consider the Korean market very important."

## Strategic rationale (Oliver's frame)

As agentic AI becomes prevalent, search, comparison, and payment will converge into **single processes where stablecoins and blockchain become essential infrastructure.**

Korea uniquely combines two factors:
1. Extensive cryptocurrency investment experience among its population (17 million investors)
2. Active AI adoption (second globally in ChatGPT spending at 5.4% of revenue)

This combination positions Korea as an **ideal real-world testing ground for blockchain-based finance before broader global implementation.**

## Doctrine implications

- "Stablecoins outside the US" — Oliver's emerging-markets-first thesis in action
- Korea as testbed before broader rollout — Visa's playbook is country-by-country experimentation
- The convergence claim: search + comparison + payment merge in agentic flow → blockchain becomes mandatory infrastructure
- 200-country lens at work: Korea is its own archetype (high-crypto + high-AI + regulatory clarity)
