# The Top Payments Predictions That Will Reshape 2026

**Author:** Oliver Jenkyn, Group President, Visa
**Date:** December 15, 2025

## Opening

> "2025 was a transformative year for payments. 2026 will eclipse it."

## The six predictions

### 1. Agentic Commerce Moves Mainstream

AI-powered shopping agents become mainstream, enabling consumers to delegate purchases through interfaces like ChatGPT. Agents can securely access tokenized payment credentials, understand personal preferences through shopping history, and respect spending parameters set by users.

> "Now an agent becomes my personal shopper."

### 2. The Fight for Identity Enters the AI Era

Criminal actors will escalate identity theft using AI-powered deepfakes and synthetic identities. Unlike historical transaction-level fraud, these attacks compromise entire identities, enabling large-scale damage. Industry collaboration will intensify to combat these threats.

> "Fraud at scale — and the damage that it leaves in its wake can be devastating."

### 3. Stablecoins Hit Their Stride

Regulatory frameworks like the U.S. **GENIUS Act** will unlock stablecoin adoption in emerging markets with unstable currencies, cross-border B2B payments, and remittances.

Visa currently supports **"more than 130 stablecoin-linked card programs in over 40 countries."**

### 4. Goodbye Manual Guest Checkout

Single-click purchases will eliminate manual card entry.

> Manual entry declined "from almost half of transactions in 2019 to just 16 percent in 2025," driven by "16 billion Visa tokens."

### 5. The Beginning of the End for Cash?

2026 marks **"the first year in history when half of the world's total consumer payments are made with card credentials."**

Cash remains prevalent (~$11 trillion globally) but faces digitization pressure.

> "Cash is King, but it is increasingly sharing its throne."

### 6. Predicting the Future Requires the Right Lens

**Market archetypes** — grouping countries by development stage and payment behaviors — reveal that geographically distant markets often share similar payment characteristics. The 200-country lens isn't 200 separate strategies; it's ~6-8 archetype clusters.

## Doctrine implications

- The "agent vs platform" framing: Visa builds the connection, model labs build the agents.
- The **GENIUS Act** is the regulatory wedge for stablecoins — emerging markets first, US/EU later.
- Card credentials win the form-factor war: 50% of consumer payments globally in 2026 = first time in history.
- 16B Visa tokens = the network effect that's eliminated manual checkout.
- Identity fraud becomes the next battleground, not transaction fraud.
