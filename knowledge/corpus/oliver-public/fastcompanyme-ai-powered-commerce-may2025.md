# Oliver Jenkyn on how Visa is building the future of AI-powered commerce

**Source:** Fast Company Middle East — May 7, 2025

## The USB port analogy

Jenkyn describes Visa's role in agentic commerce using a memorable comparison:

> "Our job isn't to build the agents. We're not the platform, we're the plug-in."

He likens Visa to a USB connection that enables seamless payment integration for AI developers building agents.

## Consumer control & delegation

Jenkyn explores how consumers will selectively delegate purchasing tasks. Everyday, low-risk items like toiletries will likely be delegated to AI agents, while personal purchases such as fashion remain under consumer control.

> "I might use generative AI to help with the initial search, but I'd want it to return to me."

## Payment infrastructure — Visa Intelligent Commerce

Visa's initiative aims to create secure payment systems supporting AI agents. The platform includes:
- Tokenized digital credentials
- Spending limits that empower consumers while protecting transactions

## New products announced

- **Flex Credential:** A single payment credential supporting credit, debit, prepaid, and other money movements
- **Visa Pay:** Bridges closed-loop domestic wallets with Visa's global network for international transactions
- **Visa Accept:** Enables small merchants to convert smartphones into payment terminals

## Future outlook

Jenkyn predicts **"AI commerce 2.0"** will dominate 2026 conversations, with early testing beginning in 2025 and broader rollouts expected by mid-year.
