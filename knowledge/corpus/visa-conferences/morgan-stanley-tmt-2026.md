# Morgan Stanley TMT Conference 2026 — Jack Forestell

Source: https://uk.investing.com/news/transcripts/visa-at-morgan-stanley-tmt-conference-embracing-ai-and-stablecoins-93CH-4538429
Date: March 3, 2026
Format: investing.com transcript / executive summary

## Key Topics & Quotes

### Product Development & AI
Forestell described Visa's architectural shift from infrastructure-focused to edge-enrichment services. On AI's impact:

> "If you're not getting better, if you're not getting faster, if your shipping velocity is not getting better, you're basically going backwards."

He highlighted rapid prototyping: a team converted a concept into working code over a weekend — work that previously took weeks or months.

### Agentic Commerce
Forestell expressed unprecedented optimism:

> "I am more excited about the scale of the opportunity in front of us with agentic commerce than anything I've seen since the advent of the internet."

He rejected zero-sum thinking, noting e-commerce added 0.75-1% GDP growth annually rather than merely substituting retail sales.

**Transaction density trends:** Average transaction size dropped 20% (from ~$55 to ~$45) over a decade, while transaction volume tripled. Agents will further densify transactions by unpacking larger purchases into smaller, optimized ones.

### Visa Trusted Agent Protocol
For agent-to-agent transactions, Visa introduced its open-standard protocol to securely identify agents and provision cryptographic credentials — enabling agent-to-agent and agent-to-merchant payments while maintaining fraud controls.

### Stablecoins
Visa positions itself as "the hyper-scaling bridge layer" for stablecoin infrastructure. Key metrics:
- 130+ stablecoin-linked card programs across 40 countries
- Settlement volume grew from $2.5B (August 2025) to $4.6B (Q1 2026) — triple-digit growth rates

Consumer demand favors card-based stablecoin payments over wallet-to-wallet transfers.

### Value-Added Services & B2B
Value-added services revenue grew 28% year-over-year to $3.2B in Q1 2026, yet penetration remains in "low single-digit %" of available market. B2B payments grew 10%, outpacing overall volume — driven by converting SMB spend and reducing friction via agentic technology, exemplified by the Ramp partnership.
