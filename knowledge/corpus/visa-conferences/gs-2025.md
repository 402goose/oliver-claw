Now, tokenization, by the way, is not as new as everyone might think. Everyone likes to think about tokenization
in the context of the digital transaction, which makes sense, but it actually really started with the card. You think
about the EMV chip, the EMV chip contains an application and that application generates a cryptogram. We
check that cryptogram once that authorization message is sent to us. That is effectively tokenization. And I bring
this up just by way of example to show you we're at almost 100% tokenized face-to-face transactions. And as
we've gotten to that level of penetration with EMV chip and contactless tokenized transactions at the point of sale,
we saw fraud reduce by almost 90%. So, that is what I always have in mind as I think about what it is that we're
doing on the digital side.

Now, we introduced digital tokenization about 10 years ago and it started out with mobile digital transactions and
